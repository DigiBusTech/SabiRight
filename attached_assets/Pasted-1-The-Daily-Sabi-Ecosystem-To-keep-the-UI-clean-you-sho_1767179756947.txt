1. The "Daily Sabi" Ecosystem
To keep the UI clean, you should group your features into three "Pillars" within the app:

Pillar 1: SabiGuard (AI Civic/Legal)

The "Legal First Aid" chatbot.

Interpreting the law based on your current situation (e.g., at a checkpoint).

Pillar 2: SabiMove (Smart Traffic & Routes)

The feature that "Sabis" the road. It updates users on "cloaked" (hidden/heavy) traffic and the safest routes to avoid checkpoints or delays.

Pillar 3: SabiWork (AI Job Search & Forum)

The filtered job board where AI matches your skills to the right roles.

The Forum: A "Civic Town Square" for valuable community discussions.

2. Updated Brand Strategy: "The Right Way, Every Day"
With these features, your brand promise shifts from just "Legal Help" to "Smart Living."

The New Tagline: > “Know the law. Beat the traffic. Get the job. SabiRight.”

3. How the Features Feed Each Other (The Business Flywheel)
This is what will impress investors. Your app has a "Circle of Value":

Traffic/Routes: Draws users in daily (High retention).

Legal AI: Provides security when they encounter trouble on those routes (High trust).

Forum/Jobs: Creates community and income (High engagement).

Marketplace: Users spend their income on Verified Pros they found in the app (Revenue).

4. Visual Design for the Super-App Layout
Since you have many features, the "Zen" part of your rebrand is critical. You need a Grid-based Dashboard (think Uber or Gojek):

Top Header: A "Route Status" bar (e.g., "Clear route to VI via Third Mainland").

Main Search: "Ask Sabi AI anything..." (Legal or Civic questions).

The 4 Big Buttons:

Legal Aid (The AI)

Smart Map (Traffic/Checkpoints)

Find Pro (Marketplace)

Jobs/Forum (Community)
